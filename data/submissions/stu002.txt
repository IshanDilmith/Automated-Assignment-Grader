Introduction
The recent transition to an AI-first customer service model was designed to streamline support. However, assessing the success of this deployment requires looking beyond operational metrics to understand how our customers actually feel about interacting with the automated system. This report analyzes recent user feedback and support interaction surveys.

Analysis of Customer Sentiment
Customer reactions to the new AI system are highly polarized, largely depending on the complexity of their issue.

Positive Feedback (The "Quick Fix"): Customers with straightforward transactional needs express high satisfaction. Survey responses frequently highlight "instant answers" and "not having to wait on hold." For routine inquiries, the AI is perceived as a significant upgrade over traditional phone queues.

Negative Feedback (The "Empathy Deficit"): Dissatisfaction spikes when customers are facing urgent or emotionally charged problems (e.g., a lost package right before a holiday). The AI's inability to recognize frustration often exacerbates the situation. Common negative phrases in survey feedback include "felt like talking to a wall" and "just wanted a real person."

The Impact on Human Agents
An unexpected secondary effect of the AI deployment is the shifting workload for our human representatives. Because the AI filters out simple queries, human agents are now dealing exclusively with complex, high-stress, or escalated issues. While call volume has dropped, the emotional toll and required effort per call have increased for our staff.

Conclusion
AI serves as an excellent triage tool and information-retrieval system, but it is not a replacement for human empathy. The current system over-prioritizes speed at the occasional expense of customer satisfaction. The company must invest in continuous training for the AI to better recognize user frustration cues and implement a faster, more obvious "route to human" button within the chat interface to preserve customer trust.
